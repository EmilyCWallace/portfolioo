Phase 1 Course Goals


1. Foster the creative habits of mind

Once I was able to grasp how to use the software and incorprorate the code, I was able to branch out and explore how to use different designing aspects. I now strategically think sbout what background color will complement my photos, or what font seems fitting for the tone of the content. I plan on expanding my abilities in this sector so I can create something truly visually satisfying for all audiences.

2. Engage with others in a workshop environment.

Engaging with my peers has allowed me to see what is possible to acheive using code. Seeing the interactive and artistic creations of my peers has encouraged me too explore and research certain code that was not provided in the index. I have also been able to learn how to make basic adjustments such as width and alignment through interacting with those around me.

3. Think deeply about craft.

With a basic understanding of how to use the style.css, I have been thinking about how to make my presentations accessable and easy to retain for any audience. I have chosen to go a simple route for most of my projects with attempts to create some subtle form of artistic expression. Now that we are moving away from informational presenting and shifting toward photography, I plan on altering my craft to be more eclectic and creative.

4. Engage in sustained research and skills development.

The reading that was assigned for the New Media Experiment allowed me to carry out fascinating research about a topic I found intriguing. The idea that new media is emerging from old media and is evolving in more forms than we know led me to want to dive into the world of hacktivism and the platforms that emerge from this. I conducted research on the hacktivist group "Annonymous" and was able to find connections with this virtual movement and the New Media movement.

5. Learn to present your work

Since this is a workshop space, I have learned to present my work honestly and to explain my intentions around my creation. I also try to explain what lies within the content and to be informative. However, I base my presentations on explaining my methods behind my creation so that I can recieve proper feedback or a tip on how to avoid or improve on a certain aspect of my creative work.
